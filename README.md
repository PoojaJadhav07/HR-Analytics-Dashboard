# HR-Analytics-Dashboard
Analyzed 1.4K+ employee records to build an HR Analytics Dashboard highlighting attrition trends, workforce demographics, and job-role patterns. Identified high-risk groups such as 26–35 age range, Life Sciences background, and Sales/Lab Technician role.

#  HR Analytics Dashboard

## 1. Project Overview
This HR Analytics Dashboard provides a complete view of employee strength, attrition behavior, demographic patterns, and job-role-wise insights.  
It helps HR teams understand **why employees leave**, **which groups are most affected**, and **where improvements are needed** for better workforce retention.

---

## 2. Key Metrics (KPIs)

### 2.1 Workforce Snapshot
- **Total Employees:** 1413  
- **Total Attrition:** 229  
- **Attrition Rate:** 16.2%  
- **Average Age:** 37 years  
- **Average Monthly Income:** 6.5K  
- **Average Years at Company:** 7 years  

**Insight:**  
An attrition rate of **16.2%** indicates moderate-to-high turnover. Employees typically stay around **7 years**, showing a need to improve retention during early-to-mid career stages.

## 4. Visual Breakdown & Insights

### 4.1 Attrition by Education
- **Life Sciences:** 38%  
- **Medical:** 25%  
- **Marketing:** 15%  
- **Technical Degree:** 14%  
- Remaining from other fields  

**Insight:**  
Employees from Life Sciences & Medical backgrounds show the highest attrition, which may indicate job-role stress or limited career progression.

---

### 4.2 Attrition by Years at Company
- Peak attrition at **1 year → 57 employees**  
- Noticeable attrition around **3–4 years (18–20 employees)**  
- Very low attrition after **10+ years**

**Insight:**  
Early exit (within 1 year) suggests onboarding or job-role mismatch issues. Attrition around 3–4 years is commonly due to stagnation or lack of promotion.

---

### 4.3 Attrition by Job Role
Top job roles with highest attrition:
1. **Laboratory Technician – 60**  
2. **Sales Executive – 55**  
3. **Research Scientist – 44**  
4. **Sales Representative – 33**

**Insight:**  
Roles involving repetitive tasks or performance pressure have higher turnover, especially Sales and Laboratory jobs.

---

### 4.4 Attrition by Age Group
- **26–35 years:** 111 employees  
- **18–25 years:** 43 employees  
- **36–45 years:** 41 employees  
- **46–55 years:** 26 employees  
- **55+ years:** 8 employees  

**Insight:**  
The **26–35 age group** has the highest attrition. This is the phase where employees actively look for better growth and salary jumps.

---

### 4.5 Job Role Matrix
A detailed table showing attrition split across:
- Job Roles  
- Age Brackets  
- Yes/No Attrition Status  

**Insight:**  
This granular breakdown helps HR identify exactly which job functions and age groups need targeted retention strategies.

---

## 5. Summary
The HR Analytics Dashboard provides a clear understanding of:
- Workforce structure  
- Attrition patterns  
- High-risk job roles  
- Vulnerable age groups  
- Education backgrounds with high turnover  

Insights reveal:
- **Early-career employees (26–35) are at highest risk**  
- **Major attrition comes from Life Sciences & Medical fields**  
- **Sales & Lab Technician roles show maximum turnover**  
- **Most employees leave within their first 1–3 years**

This dashboard helps organizations make **data-driven HR decisions** and create effective retention policies.
